Prague Czech Republic Travel

Prague is a wonderful blend of old architecture, history and modern life. You can and will find pretty much anything you could want in Prague. A booming nightlife, 1000 year old buildings, great food and quiet isolated gardents are all within easy walking distance in Old Town Prague.

A potential itinerary.

While it is entirely possible to spend only a day or two in Prague and there are plenty of videos online discussing how to have such a short trip, I feel it is a huge mistake. Your minimum trip time should be 3 full days in the Czech Republic. I would go even further and suggest that if possible your trip should be at least 3 to 5 days long.

The reason for this time frame is easy to understand. There is just so much to see and do and once you arrive you will find that you want to do them all. Even on a 5 day long trip you will need to pick what you see and what you will pass over until your next trip.


As someone who enjoys history, I found Old Town to be paradise. It is a wonderful blend of the old and new. Everywhere you go everywhere you look is something that you just feel the need to explore and see.

The Wonderful Czech Republic

  • Prague Prague is the capital of the Czech Republic and a wonderful city to visit and explore. There is no low season in Prague. They city is always busy. With that said, the best times to visit Prague are Spring and Fall. The Winter and Christmas season can also be wonderful, but cold and wet.
  • Český Krumlov is a small, charming town about 3 hours south from Prague. It has fairy-tale like qualities to it and a strong medieval feel. Český Krumlov is a great place to explore and spend a night or two. Český Krumlov castle is here and during warmer months you can hike or river raft.
  • Kutná Hora is just a quick train ride out from Prague. It is a wonderfully quiet small town with a few great cafes and restaurants. Sedlec Ossuary, or the Bone Cathedral as it more commonly known can be found here. The Bone Cathedral allows no photography inside and requires a ticket you can buy in town. Kutná Hora makes for a great day trip. Leave in the morning return late afternoon.
  • Terezín Memorial is a walled town where in 1940 Nazi Germany decided it would be a good place for a concentration camp. The camp here was more of a transit camp, holding Jews until they could be passed along to the more infamous ones in Germany and Poland. This is a very worthwhile place to visit, though it is a very somber visit. Be prepared.
  • Telč is a lovely town to visit. Great for hiking and spending a night or two here is a great plan. Telč is a quiet, beautiful small town with plenty of outdoors things to do and a castle. There are even a fair number of World War II bunkers in the area to explore. Telč is easily reached from Prague by train. I would recommend if you go, to spend the night there and return the next day to Prague.
  • Třeboň is a beautiful small town with many things to do. It is a great place to spend a day or two away from Prage. Lakes, spas, nice hotels and restaurants are some of the many things to experience here. This is a popular tourist location.
  • Mucha's Slav Epic can be found the in town of Moravský Krumlov. While there is not a lot to do in this town, the series of gigantic paintings might be worth your time, especially if you enjoy art. The epic is a series of huge pieces of art that show the history of the Slav people and civilisation.
  • Konopiště Castle is one of the most beautiful casltes in the Czech Republic to visit and is an easy day trip from Prague. For those into history this castle was also the home of the Arch Duke Franz Ferdinand. Famous from World War I times.

I am always up for spending time in Prague.

Spending time in and around Prague is one of my favorite things to do. I have been twice now for a total of 16 days and feel I have only just began to experience what it has to offer. If you are careful the cost to visit this wonderful city can be fairly reasonable compare to other cities you might visit in Europe.


I have spent 16 days in Prague and never felt in danger of crime. I suspect pick pocketing might be an issue but I did not see or hear anything on this. The Prague airport is just outside the city and it is easy to get a taxi ride to your hotel. I suggest you stay in Old Town, there are plenty of hotels there. While it can be tempting to spend your time during the day exploring and then resting at night. You might consider changing that a bit for some days, as Prague has a pretty amazing nightlife.

The Wonderful Czech Republic

  • Karlštejn Castle is a popular castle not far outside Prague. The castle itself is straight out of fantasy or fairy tales and the trip to the castle is full of wonderful views. You can get to Karlštejn Castle by train or car easily.
  • Třebíč Scenic riverside is a beautiful small city outside Prague. This is a very picturesque area and sits on a river. Spending a day and night here is a good idea. If you love history, culture and nature this is a great place for you to spend time.
  • Slavonice is a dreamy artists' retreat is in many ways a meca for those with an interest in art. The town is a lovely and with a Renaissance flair to it. The town has an active and fairly large artist population. You can find many sculptors and painters here. Slavonice is about 180 km south east from Prague so the best way to get there will be by train. Also recommend spending the night in town.
  • Karlovy Vary (Carlsbad) is known for its spas. Many europeans come here to rest, relax and heal in the many spas to be found here. It is a very relaxed and peaceful town with stunning views of nature. The architecture is mostly 19th century and if you like taking pictures of buildings you will find a great many here worth you time. It even has its own astronomical clock.
  • Olomouc. A cultural capital. Prague tends to get most of the attention of travellers and rightfully so. However, due to the history of the area, there area actually several other cultural centers and Olomouc is one of them. Olomouc is the cultural center for Moravia. With a population of about 100,000 this is a fun city to visit. Great architecture, tons of history and beautiful nature all around, you are bound to love the area. Well worth spending a night here.
  • Wallachia is an area or region and probably best explored by car or bus. There is a lot toi see and do in this beautiful mountainous and wooded area but it does tend to require the ability to stop here and there and get out of your transportation. A tour bus might be the way to go for this. It might be said that Wallachia is where Central Europe and Eastern Europe meet. The people of the area have their own dialect and you will often see people where clothing and costume matching their culture.
  • Křivoklát Castle is a Gothic castle. This castle does not have the fairy tale feel of the previous castles. Your very first sight of this castle will put you straight into the Middle Ages. If there is a spooky castle in the Czech Republic this would be it. This castle and town are very much worth visiting as it is unlike anything else in the country. This castle feels like it was made to survive dark times and if you like history at all this will be a gem for you. Spending the night in town is a great idea.
  • Mikulov Wine Region is a great place to stop and explore and maybe spend a day or two, especially if you are travelling from Prague to Vienna. It is a romantic, beautiful area with many places to eat, drink and wineries to visit. There area plenty of hotels in the area you can spend the night at and enjoy good food, great food and wine. Super awesome experience especially for couples.
